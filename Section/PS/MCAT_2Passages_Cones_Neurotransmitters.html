A monochromatic light at about 450 nm is presented at moderate intensity. Which pattern of photoreceptor response is most consistent with the figure and passage?
Explanation
The best answer is A. Around 450 nm, the S-cone curve is near its peak, while M and especially L cones are much less responsive. That means short-wavelength light should produce a response pattern dominated by S cones.
  • B is inconsistent with the graph, because medium- and long-wavelength cones are not strongest at this wavelength.
  • C is incorrect because cones do respond to short wavelengths; rods become more important mainly under low illumination, not simply because the wavelength is short.
  • D reverses the meaning of the L-cone curve, which is shifted toward longer wavelengths, not shorter ones.
Which wavelength would most likely produce the poorest discrimination based only on comparing M- and L-cone output?
Explanation
The best answer is C. When two cone classes respond almost equally, their relative output gives less information for distinguishing nearby wavelengths. The graph shows especially strong overlap of M and L cones in the mid-green to yellow range, around 545–550 nm.
  • A is less supported because if both responses are near zero, M-versus-L comparison is weak, but the question asks where discrimination based on those two outputs is poorest while still relying on their comparison, which is best captured where they overlap strongly.
  • B emphasizes S-cone dominance rather than the similarity between M and L responses.
  • D is wrong because all three cone classes are not maximal at 680 nm, and responses there are relatively low overall.
A retinal disorder selectively damages L cones while leaving S and M cones intact. Which task would be expected to be most impaired?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage explains that color vision depends on comparing activity across cone classes. In the yellow-red range, M and L cones normally both contribute strongly, so losing L cones would disrupt that comparison and impair discrimination there.
  • A and C are more rod-dependent tasks under dim conditions.
  • B would rely mainly on S-cone signaling, which is preserved.
If the same 560-nm stimulus is viewed first in bright daylight and then in very dim illumination, which change is most likely?
Explanation
The best answer is B. In bright light, color vision depends on relative cone activation. In dim light, rods contribute more because they are more sensitive to photons, but rods do not support detailed color discrimination. Thus vision becomes less color-specific.
  • A is backwards: rod dominance improves sensitivity, not color discrimination.
  • C is unsupported; rods do not directly make L cones fire more.
  • D is also unsupported because cones do not shift their spectral peaks in darkness.
Which conclusion is best supported by the overlapping cone sensitivity curves?
Explanation
The best answer is A. The passage explicitly states that color is encoded by the relative pattern of activity across multiple cone types, and the graph shows why: the curves overlap broadly.
  • B contradicts the overlap shown.
  • C is incorrect because rods mediate dim-light vision, not fine color discrimination.
  • D ignores the passage’s central point that comparison across cone outputs is necessary.
A researcher wants a wavelength that strongly activates M cones while minimizing S-cone contribution. Which choice is most appropriate?
Explanation
The best answer is D. Around 530 nm, the M-cone response is high, while the S-cone curve has already fallen to a low level. This gives strong M-cone activation with relatively little S-cone contribution.
  • A and B would strongly stimulate S cones.
  • C is too long in wavelength to strongly stimulate M cones.
In the clinical arm, a patient reports markedly reduced pain perception after receiving an experimental peptide infusion, yet shows no major change in baseline arousal or movement coordination. Which neurotransmitter system was most likely enhanced?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage identifies endorphins as endogenous analgesic molecules, so enhanced endorphin signaling best explains selective reduction in pain perception.
  • A would primarily increase excitation, not specifically analgesia.
  • B is more closely associated with mood, sleep, eating, and dreaming.
  • C is better linked to muscle activation, parasympathetic function, attention, and alertness.
In cultured cortical neurons, researchers apply a compound that selectively blocks GABAergic and glycinergic synapses. Which immediate laboratory finding would most strongly support the expected neurotransmitter effect?
Explanation
The best answer is B. GABA and glycine are major inhibitory neurotransmitters. Blocking their synapses removes stabilizing inhibition, so cortical neurons should fire more easily and show increased risk of overexcitation.
  • A predicts the opposite of losing inhibition.
  • C is too narrow and does not fit the cortical cell-culture setup.
  • D introduces catecholamines without support from the manipulation described.
A patient in the emergency department remains tachycardic, hyperalert, and unable to sleep several hours after a frightening event, despite no continuing external threat. Which neurotransmitter change most plausibly underlies the persistent state?
Explanation
The best answer is A. Norepinephrine and epinephrine are associated with fight-or-flight responses, wakefulness, and alertness. Persistent hyperarousal after the threat has passed is therefore most consistent with increased norepinephrine signaling.
  • B would reduce excitation rather than sustain hypervigilance.
  • C would increase inhibition, not produce a stress-ready state.
  • D mainly affects voluntary muscle activation and does not best explain the autonomic and arousal findings.
In a rodent movement assay, animals given Drug X show rigid posture and less fluid initiation of movement, while measures of arousal and pain sensitivity remain near baseline. Which neurotransmitter system was most likely disrupted by Drug X?
Explanation
The best answer is C. Dopamine is strongly associated with smooth movement and postural stability. A selective loss of movement fluidity with preserved arousal and pain processing most directly implicates disrupted dopaminergic signaling.
  • A is more tied to mood, sleep, eating, and dreaming.
  • B would more directly affect pain perception.
  • D does not best explain the motor-specific phenotype described.
A sleep-lab participant receives an infusion that improves mood regulation and normalizes sleep timing, but produces little change in acute nociception or gross motor coordination. Which neurotransmitter was most likely targeted?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage links serotonin to mood, sleep, eating, and dreaming. A manipulation that improves mood regulation and sleep timing is therefore best matched to serotonin.
  • A has stronger associations with muscle control, parasympathetic activity, attention, and alertness.
  • B is the major excitatory neurotransmitter, not the best fit for the selective symptom changes given.
  • C would be more likely to enhance arousal and vigilance.
During electrophysiologic recording, a cortical slice begins to show broad, synchronized depolarization after application of an unknown compound. Which additional result would most strongly support the interpretation that the compound increased the action of the brain’s main excitatory neurotransmitter?
Explanation
The best answer is B. Glutamate is the brain’s major excitatory neurotransmitter. If the unknown compound increases glutamatergic action, the strongest supporting result would be greater postsynaptic activation accompanying widespread depolarization.
  • A suggests more inhibition, not more excitation.
  • C points toward endorphins rather than glutamate.
  • D is more consistent with cholinergic effects.
