A pigeon’s cumulative record shows a very steep line with almost no pauses, even though reinforcement remains intermittent and unpredictable. Which schedule from the figure most likely produced this pattern?
Explanation
The best answer is B. Variable ratio schedules produce high, steady response rates because reinforcement depends on responding and the exact requirement is unpredictable.
  • A usually produces a scalloped pattern, not a continuously steep line.
  • C tends to produce a moderate, steady slope rather than the steepest responding.
  • D often shows brief post-reinforcement pauses, unlike the nearly pause-free pattern described.
A cumulative record repeatedly shows short bursts of rapid responding followed by brief flat pauses immediately after each reinforcement delivery. Which inference is most consistent with the graph?
Explanation
The best answer is D. A fixed ratio schedule typically yields rapid responding separated by post-reinforcement pauses, creating the stair-step appearance seen in cumulative records.
  • A describes a variable interval schedule.
  • B points toward interval schedules in general rather than the ratio pattern with pauses.
  • C is more characteristic of variable ratio responding, which is usually steadier and less pause-prone.
Suppose a researcher wants moderate but relatively steady responding without the pronounced pauses seen after reinforcement on some schedules. Based on the figure, which schedule would best fit that goal?
Explanation
The best answer is A. Variable interval schedules usually produce moderate, fairly steady rates of responding because time, not sheer response count, determines when reinforcement becomes available.
  • B tends to produce scalloping, with slow responding after reinforcement and acceleration later.
  • C commonly includes post-reinforcement pauses.
  • D produces high steady responding, but the stem asks for a more moderate rate.
On one schedule, the cumulative record is relatively flat just after reinforcement, then curves upward as time passes before the next reinforcer becomes available. What is the strongest interpretation of this pattern?
Explanation
The best answer is C. The scalloped fixed interval pattern reflects low responding just after reinforcement and acceleration as the organism nears the expected time when the next reinforcer can be earned.
  • A better matches variable ratio responding.
  • B describes fixed ratio pauses, not the smooth time-based acceleration shown here.
  • D is not supported by the cumulative record.
If reinforcement were changed from a fixed ratio schedule to a variable ratio schedule while all else stayed constant, which change in the cumulative record would be most likely?
Explanation
The best answer is A. Variable ratio schedules maintain high, steady responding with less predictable pausing, so the fixed-ratio stair-step pattern should smooth into a steeper, more continuous line.
  • B is incorrect because the comparison is between two ratio schedules, not interval versus ratio.
  • C describes fixed interval performance.
  • D cannot occur on a cumulative record because the total number of responses never decreases.
A student claims that the fixed interval curve must represent the highest motivation because it accelerates sharply before reinforcement. Which response best evaluates that claim using the graph?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The fixed interval curve accelerates late in each interval, but its overall responding is less steep and less sustained than the variable ratio pattern, which better reflects consistently high response rate.
  • A incorrectly equates curvature with maximal reinforcement or motivation.
  • B is false; ratio schedules, especially variable ratio, commonly generate higher rates.
  • C is wrong because the graph explicitly plots cumulative responses over time.
A patient arrives in the emergency department after head trauma, and clinicians need a rapid structural scan to assess for acute bleeding and gross tissue disruption. Which brain-mapping method is most appropriate first?
Explanation
The best answer is C. CT uses multiple X-rays to create cross-sectional structural images and is especially useful when clinicians need rapid structural assessment.
  • A records electrical activity rather than giving fast structural imaging.
  • B is functional or metabolic and is not the best first test for this urgent structural question.
  • D is used intraoperatively for cortical mapping, not initial trauma screening.
During an epilepsy study, investigators want millisecond-level information about when abnormal neural firing begins and spreads across cortical networks. Which method would best provide that kind of data noninvasively?
Explanation
The best answer is B. EEG records electrical activity from large populations of neurons with excellent temporal resolution and is commonly used for seizures and sleep studies.
  • A provides structural detail, not the best timing information about neural firing.
  • C is invasive and mainly a lesion method for animal research.
  • D reflects blood flow and is slower than direct electrical recording for timing seizure spread.
In a lab-animal study, researchers want to create a highly localized lesion in a deep brain nucleus while minimizing damage to neighboring tissue. Which additional tool is most important for achieving that goal?
Explanation
The best answer is D. Stereotactic instruments allow highly precise three-coordinate placement of electrodes or lesioning tools, which is essential for selectively targeting deep structures.
  • A records broad surface electrical activity and cannot guide deep lesion placement.
  • B describes PET-like functional imaging, not precise lesion targeting.
  • C lacks the precision required by the stem.
During awake neurosurgery, stimulation of a cortical site causes the patient to report seeing flashes of light that are not actually present. Which conclusion is most justified?
Explanation
The best answer is A. Awake cortical stimulation can transiently activate the functions associated with the stimulated neurons, so eliciting flashes of light suggests involvement in visual cortical processing.
  • B is anatomically and functionally incorrect.
  • C goes far beyond what the observation supports.
  • D is wrong because the passage explicitly describes perceptual effects of stimulation.
A cognitive neuroscience lab wants to compare which cortical regions become more active while volunteers listen to music versus spoken language. Which method best matches the goal described in the passage?
Explanation
The best answer is C. fMRI measures activity-related changes in blood flow, making it well suited for comparing regional activation during different cognitive tasks.
  • A is structural and does not directly track function this way.
  • B is too narrow for mapping broad cortical regions across a task.
  • D is invasive and not appropriate for this purpose.
Why are naturally occurring human lesion studies often less definitive than experimentally produced lesions in laboratory animals when researchers try to map a brain function to one structure?
Explanation
The best answer is B. Human lesions are often not cleanly isolated to a single structure, so impaired function may reflect damage to any of several affected regions.
  • A is false; lesions can often be visualized.
  • C is the opposite of the passage, which emphasizes stereotactic precision in animals.
  • D is false because animal lesion studies are also paired with behavioral observation.
