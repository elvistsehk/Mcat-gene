Cell Cycle
Timer 13:00
Passage 1 (Questions 1–8)

A breast oncology group designed a short neoadjuvant study to ask a cell-cycle question rather than a survival question: which endocrine regimen most quickly pushes tumor cells out of active cycling before surgery? They therefore tracked Ki-67, a nuclear marker that is generally present in cells moving through G1, S, G2, and M, but is low or absent in quiescent G0 cells. The investigators warned trainees not to overinterpret this marker. A reduction in Ki-67 does not by itself prove that cells have died; it can also mean that fewer cells are crossing the restriction point in late G1 and committing to DNA synthesis.

Table 1. Mean suppression of Ki-67 for anastrozole, tamoxifen, and the combination after 2 weeks and 12 weeks, sorted by HER2 expression.

To connect endocrine signaling to cell-cycle control, the team focused on the estrogen-responsive cyclin D–CDK4/6 pathway. In many ER-positive tumors, estrogen signaling promotes progression through G1 toward S phase. Anastrozole lowers estrogen production, whereas tamoxifen blocks estrogen receptor signaling at the receptor level. Because both interventions can reduce entry into the cell cycle, a naive prediction would be that combining them must always produce the greatest Ki-67 suppression. The investigators cautioned that this conclusion is too simple: if two drugs converge on overlapping signaling nodes, their effects need not be additive in every biologic context.

Figure 2. Mean suppression of Ki-67 for anastrozole (A), tamoxifen (B), and both (C), after 2 weeks and 12 weeks, sorted by ER expression.

The team also considered bypass pathways. HER2 signaling can sustain proliferation through growth-factor pathways that maintain cyclin activity even when estrogen signaling is reduced. If that is true, an early fall in Ki-67 might not be maintained in HER2-positive tumors, especially when the tumor can reroute signaling and re-enter the cycle. By contrast, tumors with stronger ER dependence should show deeper endocrine sensitivity, particularly when patients are stratified by ER expression. Figure 2 therefore matters because it tests whether endocrine responsiveness tracks with ER abundance, which would support a hormone-driven G1 arrest model.

Figure 1. Ki-67 expression before and after two weeks of treatment with anastrozole (A), tamoxifen (B), or both (C).

Finally, the investigators warned against relying only on group means. Window studies are short, and a mean reduction can hide clinically important heterogeneity. In paired plots, a regimen may appear effective overall even if a subgroup of tumors shows little change or even an increase in Ki-67. For this reason, the team reviewed both the summary plots and the individual trajectories before inferring which treatment most convincingly induces cell-cycle exit.

How to reason

  • Start by separating what Ki-67 means from why it changed. A lower value suggests fewer actively cycling cells, but the passage warns that it does not automatically prove cell death.
  • For any item comparing regimens, connect the graph to the passage’s G1-to-S transition model. Ask which treatment pattern best fits early cell-cycle arrest and which pattern suggests escape from that arrest.
  • When HER2 appears in a question, test the idea of bypass signaling. Look for cases in which suppression is present early but is not maintained later, because that supports re-entry into the cycle through an alternate mitogenic route.
  • When ER quartiles are used, decide whether the data show greater response in tumors with stronger hormone dependence. Use that trend together with the passage, not the graph alone.
  • Do not trust averages by themselves. Use the paired trajectories in Figure 1 to check for heterogeneity, including outliers or tumors that move in the wrong direction despite a favorable mean.
1. A fellow argues that HER2-positive tumors may initially respond to estrogen deprivation but later restore cell-cycle progression through an alternative mitogenic pathway. Which observation most strongly supports that interpretation?
Explanation
The best answer is A. The passage predicts that bypass signaling would look like loss of sustained suppression: cells are initially pushed out of cycling, but later re-enter. Table 1 shows exactly that pattern for HER2+ anastrozole, where suppression is strong at 2 weeks but much weaker at 12 weeks. The comparison with HER2− tumors matters because it suggests the effect is not just a general time effect; it is consistent with HER2-linked escape from endocrine control.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • B: This supports ER dependence, not HER2-mediated escape.
  • C: Baseline heterogeneity does not show recovery of cycling after treatment.
  • D: Stable suppression over time argues against escape, not for it.
2. A resident claims that because anastrozole and tamoxifen both reduce estrogen-driven proliferation, the combination must outperform either single agent in every subtype. Which result most directly contradicts that claim?
Explanation
The best answer is C. The passage warns that drugs acting on overlapping signaling nodes need not be additive. Figure 2 directly tests that idea across ER strata, and in the highest ER quartile at 12 weeks, anastrozole alone suppresses Ki-67 more than the combination. That single counterexample is enough to refute the claim that combination therapy must always be best.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: A decrease with the combination does not show it is worse than a single agent.
  • B: This compares different biologic subtypes and different regimens, so it does not directly test whether combining the two drugs is always superior.
  • D: Baseline spread says nothing about whether the combination consistently outperforms both single agents.
3. The team wants a short 2-week presurgical trial that is most likely to demonstrate rapid endocrine-induced cell-cycle arrest. Based on the passage and the figures, which cohort would be the best choice?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage links endocrine sensitivity to ER dependence and interprets a sharp early Ki-67 fall as evidence of rapid G1 arrest. Figure 2 shows that anastrozole produces the strongest 2-week suppression in the highest ER quartile, making that cohort the clearest test of rapid endocrine-induced withdrawal from the cell cycle.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: The figure shows weaker, not stronger, suppression when ER expression is lower.
  • B: The passage predicts HER2 may permit escape, and Table 1 does not suggest HER2+ disease is the cleanest endocrine-response cohort.
  • C: Generalizability is not the study goal; the question asks which group most clearly demonstrates early arrest.
4. A pathologist says the marked 2-week Ki-67 drop in many anastrozole-treated tumors proves that the drug caused widespread tumor-cell death. Which additional finding would best support the investigators’ more cautious interpretation instead?
Explanation
The best answer is B. Figure 1 shows that many tumors have a large fall in Ki-67 after 2 weeks, but the passage explicitly warns that this indicates fewer actively cycling cells, not necessarily apoptosis. A shift toward G0/G1 accumulation and reduced S phase fits the model of cells failing to cross the late-G1 restriction point rather than dying outright.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: That would support cell death, which is the interpretation the investigators specifically caution against.
  • C: More mitoses would contradict the idea of cell-cycle suppression.
  • D: If cell-cycle distribution is unchanged, it would not explain the biologically meaningful fall in Ki-67.
5. Why did the investigators insist on reviewing Figure 1 in addition to the mean summaries in Table 1 and Figure 2?
Explanation
The best answer is B. The passage warns that a favorable group mean can conceal heterogeneity. In Figure 1, most lines trend downward, but not all do so to the same degree, and a few tumors show minimal suppression or even upward movement. That is exactly why paired individual trajectories are informative in a short window study.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: The figure does not show a calculation error.
  • C: HER2 status is not identifiable from the paired-line plot.
  • D: Baseline spread exists, but the figure does not prove baseline alone determines response.
6. A lab member concludes that ER abundance matters only after prolonged therapy, not during the first 2 weeks. Which statement best rebuts that conclusion?
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage predicts that tumors with stronger ER-driven proliferation should show stronger endocrine response. Figure 2 already shows a clear 2-week gradient by ER quartile, particularly in the anastrozole arm, so ER abundance is influencing response early, not only after prolonged treatment.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: Figure 1 is not stratified by ER quartile, so it cannot answer the claim.
  • B: That compares HER2 groups, not ER abundance at 2 weeks.
  • C: Similarity at 12 weeks does not address whether ER matters during the first 2 weeks.
7. Suppose a new tumor subtype shows a large 2-week Ki-67 reduction after endocrine therapy, followed by little additional change from 2 to 12 weeks. Which cell-cycle interpretation best fits the investigators’ framework?
Explanation
The best answer is C. The passage links endocrine therapy to reduced cyclin D–CDK4/6 signaling, which controls progression through G1 toward S phase. If most of the Ki-67 decrease appears by 2 weeks and then plateaus, the most coherent inference is that cells are being prevented from making the early commitment to DNA synthesis, so the major drop in proliferating cells occurs quickly.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: Anaphase is far downstream of the endocrine mechanism proposed in the passage.
  • B: Nothing in the passage or figures implicates direct S-phase enzymatic inhibition.
  • D: The investigators explicitly warn not to equate lower Ki-67 with cell death.
8. A clinician argues that the weaker long-term suppression in HER2+ disease could be dismissed as a sample-size artifact from one small subgroup. Which response is best supported by the evidence presented?
Explanation
The best answer is B. Small sample size should always prompt caution, but the pattern in Table 1 is not confined to a single HER2+ cell of the table. Across multiple HER2+ treatment arms, the 12-week suppression values converge in the mid-40% range, while HER2− tumors generally show stronger sustained suppression. In the passage’s framework, that recurring pattern is more consistent with a biologic escape mechanism than with a one-off fluctuation.
Why the other choices are not as good
  • A: The table addresses treatment response, not just baseline.
  • C: Figure 1 is not stratified by HER2 status.
  • D: ER and HER2 measure different biologic axes and are not shown to be interchangeable.
