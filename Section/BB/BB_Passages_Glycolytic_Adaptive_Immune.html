Question 1
Reasoning Within the Text
Which interpretation best explains why the investigators emphasize both the ATP time-course and the PI-staining results before arguing that 3BrPA preferentially targets malignant cells?
Explanation
The best answer is B. A fall in ATP suggests that glycolysis is being functionally disrupted, but ATP loss alone does not establish whether cells actually become nonviable. The PI assay addresses that downstream consequence by showing membrane-compromised, nonviable cells. When malignant cells show both a stronger ATP decline and a larger increase in PI positivity, the combined evidence supports preferential vulnerability.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A is backwards: ATP data do not prove membrane integrity is unchanged.
  • C is too specific; PI staining cannot identify the exact inhibited enzyme.
  • D overstates the data. PBMCs are affected less, but the figures do not prove they never use glycolysis.
Question 2
Reasoning Beyond the Text
Suppose a new malignant cell line shows almost no ATP decrease after 50 μM 3BrPA, yet still becomes highly PI-positive after treatment. Which conclusion would be most justified?
Explanation
The best answer is D. If ATP remains relatively stable while PI positivity rises, then the death pathway in that new line may not follow the same early energy-collapse pattern observed in RPMI8226. That would suggest mechanistic heterogeneity rather than invalidate PI staining or all cancer use of the drug.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A is unsupported because PI positivity still indicates loss of viability in the described assay.
  • B is contradicted by the hypothetical, which specifically breaks that pattern.
  • C is far too broad; one divergent cell line would not eliminate all possible therapeutic utility.
Question 3
Foundations of Comprehension
Which feature of the data most directly supports the claim that normal PBMCs are less sensitive than RPMI8226 cells to glycolytic inhibition at comparable inhibitor concentrations?
Explanation
The best answer is A. A selective-sensitivity argument depends on comparing the same treatment across cell types. At 30 μM, the malignant RPMI8226 cells are much more PI-positive than PBMC1, showing substantially greater death at a comparable dose.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • B describes a design difference but does not itself establish lower PBMC sensitivity.
  • C may reflect testing range, but it does not directly prove relative sensitivity at comparable doses.
  • D is true of many assays and does not support the selectivity claim.
Question 4
Reasoning Within the Text
The statistical brackets in the ATP comparison are most useful because they help the reader distinguish which of the following?
Explanation
The best answer is C. Statistical brackets usually mark pairwise comparisons judged significant, helping the reader identify where the strongest evidence for a real between-group difference lies. Here, they guide interpretation of malignant versus normal-cell ATP effects under treatment.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A concerns axis definition, not significance brackets.
  • B is a mechanistic transport question not answered by the bracket notation.
  • D concerns timing, which is not what these brackets denote.
Question 5
Reasoning Beyond the Text
If the investigators wanted to test whether the ATP decline after 3BrPA truly reflects inhibition of glycolytic ATP production rather than a general assay artifact, which additional result would be most supportive?
Explanation
The best answer is A. A mechanistic rescue is strong evidence: if ATP loss is specifically tied to blocked glycolytic ATP production, then shifting cells toward another ATP source or otherwise bypassing the blocked pathway should blunt the fall.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • B only shows that detergents can kill cells; it says nothing about glycolysis.
  • C speaks to baseline consistency, not mechanism.
  • D may indicate some cellular effect, but it does not test whether glycolytic inhibition caused the ATP result.
Question 6
Foundations of Comprehension
Based on the passage definition of PI staining, an increase in PI-positive cells after treatment most directly indicates an increase in:
Explanation
The best answer is D. The passage explicitly states that PI enters cells with disrupted membrane integrity and therefore marks nonviable cells more strongly. So higher PI positivity means more cells have lost viability.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A is unrelated to what PI staining measures.
  • B cannot be inferred from PI uptake alone.
  • C is not what the dye reports.
Question 7
Reasoning Beyond the Text
Taken together, the figures best support which overall conclusion about the potential use of 3BrPA in this experimental system?
Explanation
The best answer is A. The time-course, cross-cell-type ATP comparison, and PI results together support preferential vulnerability of the malignant cells in this system. However, the data do not justify stronger universal claims about complete normal-tissue safety or every cancer type.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • B overclaims; PBMCs are less affected, not completely unaffected.
  • C contradicts the figures, which associate treatment with ATP decline.
  • D is far broader than the data allow.
Question 8
Reasoning Within the Text
Which event is most directly responsible for the stronger and faster antibody rise observed after the booster dose?
Explanation
The best answer is C. A secondary response is defined by being faster and more vigorous than the primary response, which is exactly what memory B-cells and memory helper T-cells are built to produce.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A occurs during T-cell development and is important for self-tolerance, not booster kinetics.
  • B naïve B-cells do not secrete specific antibodies before activation.
  • D describes passive immunity, which provides immediate protection but does not explain the host's boosted secondary response.
Question 9
Foundations of Comprehension
In the vaccine scenario described, helper T-cells are activated when they recognize antigen presented on:
Explanation
The best answer is B. CD4+ helper T-cells recognize peptide antigen presented on MHC II, typically by professional antigen-presenting cells such as dendritic cells.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A helper T-cells do not recognize antigen on MHC I; that is the domain of CD8+ cytotoxic T-cells, and erythrocytes do not present antigen this way.
  • C free antibody is not the presentation platform for helper T-cells.
  • D complement can assist immunity but does not replace MHC II presentation to CD4+ cells.
Question 10
Reasoning Beyond the Text
Why does the passage describe passive immunization as producing immediate short-term protection but little long-term memory by itself?
Explanation
The best answer is D. Passive immunization works by giving the person ready-made antibodies, so protection appears immediately. But because the host may not mount its own full adaptive response, little durable immunologic memory is generated unless active immunization also occurs.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A is not how passive antibodies work.
  • B overstates the effect; dendritic cells can still encounter antigen.
  • C is inaccurate because passive immunization is about antibodies, not selective CD8 activation.
Question 11
Reasoning Within the Text
The observation that some CD8+ T-cell activation occurs despite administration of an exogenous protein antigen is best explained by:
Explanation
The best answer is A. The passage explicitly notes that dendritic cells can cross-present exogenous antigen on MHC I, allowing priming of CD8+ T-cells even when the original antigen came from outside the cell.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • B and C are B-cell processes and do not explain CD8 activation.
  • D is important for tolerance, but it does not account for this vaccine-induced response.
Question 12
Reasoning Beyond the Text
A patient later develops an immune response against a normal host protein that resembles the viral antigen used in the vaccine. This outcome would be most consistent with failure of:
Explanation
The best answer is C. When the immune system attacks a self-antigen, the central problem is breakdown of self-tolerance, which underlies autoimmune disease.

Why the others are less appropriate:
  • A changes binding quality but does not by itself define autoimmunity.
  • B changes antibody class, not the distinction between self and nonself.
  • D is one antibody function, not the regulatory failure being tested.
