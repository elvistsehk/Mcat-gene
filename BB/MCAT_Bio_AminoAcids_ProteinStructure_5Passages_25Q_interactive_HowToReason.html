📘 Passage 1 - Amino acid identity, chirality, and side-chain chemistry

A researcher is designing a short peptide to insert into a lipid membrane and remain stable in aqueous buffer. The design includes several residues with long alkyl side chains as well as a single residue that can form a covalent cross-link under oxidizing conditions.

During synthesis, the lab accidentally supplies one batch of amino acids in the D-configuration. The peptide is still produced, but its interaction with enzymes and its folding behavior appear altered compared with the all‑L version typically found in eukaryotic proteins.

The team also notes that one amino acid in the sequence is not chiral because its R group is a hydrogen atom.

How to reason:

  1. Extract the design goal: membrane insertion favors hydrophobic residues; stability via oxidation suggests disulfide formation.
  2. Map residues to properties: Val/Leu/Ile are strongly hydrophobic; Cys forms disulfide bonds; Gly is achiral.
  3. Use stereochemistry logic: eukaryotic proteins are built from L-amino acids; introducing D-amino acids can alter folding and protease recognition.
  4. Choose answers that connect side-chain chemistry → location (membrane vs aqueous) and reactivity (disulfides) rather than memorized labels.
1. Which set of residues would most strongly favor insertion into the hydrophobic core of a lipid bilayer?
A. Valine, leucine, isoleucine
B. Aspartate, glutamate, lysine
C. Serine, threonine, asparagine
D. Arginine, histidine, glutamate
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Nonpolar aliphatic side chains (Val, Leu, Ile) are hydrophobic and partition into membranes more readily than polar or charged residues.
中: V/L/I 係非極性長烷基側鏈，疏水，最易插入脂雙層疏水核心；帶電/極性側鏈會偏向水相。
2. Which amino acid is nonchiral because its R group is a hydrogen atom?
A. Alanine
B. Glycine
C. Cysteine
D. Valine
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Glycine has H as its side chain, so the α-carbon has two identical substituents (two H) and is not chiral.
中: Glycine 側鏈=H，α-碳有兩個 H（不再是四個不同取代基），所以唔手性。
3. The presence of D-amino acids is most strongly associated with which statement?
A. They are required for all eukaryotic ribosomes to translate mRNA
B. They are the predominant form of amino acids in human proteins
C. They can occur in prokaryotes and may reduce susceptibility to common proteases
D. They cannot exist because amino acids are always L in all organisms
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Eukaryotic proteins are built from L-amino acids, but prokaryotes can contain D-amino acids (e.g., in cell wall components), which may resist enzymes tailored to L-peptides.
中: 真核蛋白多用 L；但原核可出現 D-胺基酸（如細菌結構），而且可令常見蛋白酶（多針對 L）較難分解。
4. Which chiral amino acid is the exception to the rule that most L-amino acids have (S) configuration?
A. Leucine
B. Serine
C. Tryptophan
D. Cysteine
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Cysteine is the classic exception: despite being L, it is (R) by CIP rules due to the sulfur-containing side chain affecting priority ranking.
中: Cys 係經典例外：雖然係 L，但因為硫原子優先序改變，CIP 配置變成 (R)，唔係 (S)。
5. Which residue enables a covalent cross-link under oxidizing conditions via disulfide bond formation?
A. Cysteine
B. Proline
C. Phenylalanine
D. Asparagine
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect

中: 兩個 Cys 的 –SH 可被氧化形成 S–S 共價鍵（cystine），就係常見嘅蛋白交聯。
📘 Passage 2 - Titration behavior and isoelectric point

A student titrates an amino acid that has no ionizable side chain. At very low pH, the amino acid exists predominantly in a fully protonated form. As base is added, a buffering region appears near one pKa, followed later by a second buffering region.

The student notices that the titration curve is relatively flat near each pKa but becomes steep near a particular pH at which the net charge of the amino acid is zero. The lab uses this pH to predict migration in an electric field.

In a different trial, the student titrates an amino acid with an additional ionizable side chain and observes three buffering regions.

How to reason:

  1. Identify what the curve features mean: flat regions indicate buffering near a pKa; steep regions indicate low buffering capacity.
  2. At pH = pKa, apply Henderson–Hasselbalch: [HA] = [A−].
  3. Find the pI (net charge 0): for amino acids without ionizable side chains, average the two backbone pKas (α-COOH and α-NH3+).
  4. For acidic/basic side chains, recognize an extra ionizable group creates an additional buffering region and shifts the pI lower (acidic) or higher (basic).
6. At a pH equal to the pKa of an ionizable group, which relationship is correct?
A. The group is fully protonated
B. [HA] = [A−] (half protonated, half deprotonated)
C. The net charge of the amino acid must be +1
D. The group is fully deprotonated
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: By definition, pH = pKa when half of the species is deprotonated and [HA] equals [A−].
中: 定義上 pH = pKa 時，一半去質子化，所以 [HA] = [A−]。
7. At very low (acidic) pH, an amino acid without a charged side chain is most likely to exist as:
A. NH2 and COO− (fully deprotonated)
B. NH2 and COOH (neutral, no charges)
C. NH3+ and COOH (fully protonated)
D. NH3+ and COO− (zwitterion as the only form)
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Low pH supplies abundant H+, so the amino group is protonated (NH3+) and the carboxyl group remains COOH.
中: 酸性低 pH 有大量 H+，所以胺基變 NH3+、羧基保持 COOH，屬「全質子化」。
8. For an amino acid with no ionizable side chain, the isoelectric point (pI) is calculated by:
A. Subtracting the two pKa values
B. Adding all pKa values and dividing by three
C. Using only the side-chain pKa
D. Averaging the two pKa values of the α-carboxyl and α-amino groups
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Without an ionizable side chain, the neutral zwitterion lies between deprotonation of the carboxyl and amino groups, so pI is the average of those two pKa values.
中: 冇可電離側鏈時，zwitterion（淨電荷 0）介乎兩個主鏈 pKa 之間，所以 pI = (pKa1 + pKa2)/2。
9. The titration curve is most nearly vertical near the pI because:
A. Small additions of titrant rapidly change net charge around the zwitterion point, producing a steep pH change
B. Buffering capacity is maximal at pI
C. [HA] always equals [A−] at pI for every ionizable group
D. The amino acid is completely insoluble at pI
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Buffering is strongest near pKa (flat regions). Near pI, the molecule transitions through net charge 0, so pH changes steeply with added titrant (vertical region).
中: pKa 附近先係最平（buffering 強）。pI 附近係淨電荷轉換區，滴少少鹼/酸都令 pH 大變，所以曲線接近垂直。
10. Compared with amino acids lacking ionizable side chains (pI ~6), acidic amino acids (Asp/Glu) generally have:
A. A much higher pI because they carry extra positive charge
B. A lower pI because they acquire negative charge more readily
C. An unchanged pI because side chains do not affect net charge
D. No pI because they cannot form zwitterions
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Acidic side chains introduce an additional carboxyl group, so the amino acid becomes negatively charged at lower pH, shifting pI below ~6.
中: 酸性側鏈多一個羧基，更易帶負電，因此淨電荷=0 的點會向低 pH 移，pI 低於 6。
📘 Passage 3 - Peptide bond chemistry and rigidity

In a synthesis reaction, a dipeptide is formed by linking two amino acids. The reaction produces a small molecule byproduct and proceeds when the nucleophilic amino group of one amino acid attacks the electrophilic carbonyl carbon of another.

When the resulting peptide is analyzed, the bond between the carbonyl carbon and the amide nitrogen appears unusually rigid, restricting rotation. Later, an enzyme catalyzes the reverse reaction in water to break the peptide into its original amino acids.

How to reason:

  1. Classify the forward reaction: peptide bond formation is a condensation (dehydration) producing water as a byproduct.
  2. Track electrophile/nucleophile: the amino nitrogen attacks the carbonyl carbon to form an amide (peptide bond).
  3. Explain rigidity: resonance gives the C–N bond partial double-bond character, restricting rotation.
  4. Reverse reaction: cleavage in water is hydrolysis, regenerating free amino acids.
11. Formation of a peptide bond between two amino acids is best classified as:
A. Hydrolysis that consumes water
B. Redox reaction that transfers electrons between side chains
C. Condensation (dehydration) reaction that releases water
D. Isomerization that changes stereochemistry without bond formation
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Peptide bond formation is a dehydration (condensation) reaction: the amino acid linkage forms while releasing a molecule of water.
中: 肽鍵形成屬縮合/脫水反應：成鍵同時放出一分子水。
12. In peptide bond formation, which functional group acts as the nucleophile and which site is electrophilic?
A. Carboxyl oxygen is nucleophile; amino nitrogen is electrophile
B. R group is nucleophile; α-carbon is electrophile
C. Hydrogen is nucleophile; carboxyl carbon is electrophile
D. Amino nitrogen is nucleophile; carbonyl carbon is electrophile
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: The amino group (nitrogen lone pair) is nucleophilic and attacks the electrophilic carbonyl carbon to form the amide (peptide) bond.
中: 胺基 N 有孤對電子→親核；羰基碳帶部分正電→親電；N 進攻 C=O 形成酰胺（肽鍵）。
13. Why is the peptide (amide) bond relatively rigid compared with a typical single bond?
A. Resonance gives the C–N bond partial double-bond character, limiting rotation
B. The peptide bond is purely ionic, so rotation is impossible
C. The peptide bond contains a full triple bond between C and N
D. Hydrophobic interactions lock the peptide bond in place
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Amide resonance delocalizes electrons between the carbonyl and nitrogen, giving the C–N bond partial double-bond character and restricting rotation.
中: 酰胺共振令 C–N 有部分雙鍵性，因此轉動受限，肽鍵較硬。
14. Breaking a peptide bond to regenerate free amino acids is best described as:
A. Condensation reaction releasing water
B. Hydrolysis reaction consuming water
C. Deamination reaction removing an amino group
D. Phosphorylation reaction adding phosphate
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Peptide bond cleavage is hydrolysis: water is used to break the amide linkage and restore carboxyl and amino groups.
中: 斬肽鍵係水解：用水拆開酰胺鍵，還原返羧基同胺基。
15. A peptide containing 15 amino acid residues is most appropriately termed a(n):
A. Dipeptide
B. Tripeptide
C. Oligopeptide
D. Polypeptide
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Oligopeptides contain a 'few' residues (often <20). Polypeptides are typically >20 residues.
中: 15 個 residues 屬「少量」(<20) → oligopeptide；>20 先多數叫 polypeptide。
📘 Passage 4 - Secondary structure and the role of backbone hydrogen bonding

A biochemist compares two peptides that share the same primary sequence except for one substitution. In the first peptide, the sequence forms a stable α-helix. In the second peptide, a single residue introduces a sharp bend and disrupts the helical pattern.

Further analysis reveals that secondary structure stability depends largely on hydrogen bonds between backbone amide N–H groups and backbone carbonyl oxygens, rather than interactions between side chains alone. The lab also examines β-pleated sheets and notes that strands can be parallel or antiparallel.

How to reason:

  1. Locate the key stabilizer of secondary structure: backbone hydrogen bonds between C=O and N–H, not primarily side-chain bonds.
  2. Predict helix disruption: proline introduces a kink and lacks a backbone N–H for helix H-bonding, so it often breaks α-helices.
  3. Differentiate motifs: α-helices are typically right-handed; β-sheets are extended strands arranged in parallel or antiparallel orientation.
  4. Pick answers that tie a structural change (single substitution) to predictable shifts in H-bond geometry and backbone constraints.
16. Which statement best defines primary protein structure?
A. Clockwise coiling stabilized by hydrogen bonds
B. Association of multiple subunits into a functional complex
C. Overall 3D folding driven primarily by hydrophobic collapse
D. Linear amino acid sequence stabilized by peptide bonds
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Primary structure is the linear sequence of amino acids linked by peptide bonds.
中: 一級結構＝胺基酸線性排列順序，靠肽鍵（共價）連住。
17. The key stabilizing interaction in both α-helices and β-sheets is:
A. Hydrogen bonding between backbone carbonyl oxygens and backbone amide hydrogens
B. Covalent disulfide bonds between any two residues
C. Ionic bonding between peptide bonds and water molecules
D. π–π stacking between every adjacent residue
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Secondary structure is stabilized primarily by hydrogen bonds between backbone C=O and N–H groups (not primarily by side-chain covalent bonds).
中: 二級結構主要靠主鏈 C=O 與 N–H 之間嘅氫鍵穩定（唔係靠側鏈共價鍵）。
18. Which substitution most likely caused the sharp bend that disrupts an α-helix?
A. Alanine → valine
B. Leucine → proline
C. Serine → threonine
D. Aspartate → glutamate
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Proline’s rigid cyclic structure and lack of a backbone N–H (for helix H-bonding) often introduces kinks and disrupts α-helices.
中: Proline 環狀剛性高，而且主鏈缺少可供氫鍵嘅 N–H，常造成 kink，打斷 α-helix。
19. In naturally occurring proteins, α-helices are typically described as:
A. Random coils with no repeating pattern
B. Left-handed helices around a central axis
C. Right-handed (clockwise) coils around a central axis

EN: α-helices in proteins are usually right-handed (clockwise) coils stabilized by backbone hydrogen bonding.
中: 蛋白入面 α-helix 通常係右手（順時針）螺旋，靠主鏈氫鍵穩定。
20. β-pleated sheets are best described as:
A. Structures stabilized by hydrogen bonds between side chains only
B. Clockwise coils that pack tightly in the protein core
C. Single rigid bonds that cannot hydrogen bond
D. Rippled strands that can be arranged in parallel or antiparallel orientation

❌Incorrect

EN: β-sheets consist of extended strands; neighboring strands hydrogen bond and can align in parallel or antiparallel fashion.

📘 Passage 5 - Folding forces, subunits, and denaturation

A globular enzyme folds so that many nonpolar side chains become buried in its interior while polar and charged side chains remain exposed to water. The lab measures that folding is favored even though ordering the polypeptide chain seems to reduce entropy.

The protein contains two cysteine residues that form an intramolecular covalent bond after oxidation. Another version of the enzyme functions only when a tightly bound metal ion remains attached, suggesting a conjugated protein with a prosthetic group.

When the enzyme is heated or placed in a highly concentrated solute solution, it loses its three-dimensional shape and catalytic activity, even though its peptide bonds remain intact.

How to reason:

  1. Use the thermodynamics cue: burying hydrophobics increases water entropy (hydrophobic effect), helping make folding favorable (ΔG < 0).
  2. Identify covalent stabilization: oxidized cysteines form a disulfide bond (cystine).
  3. Match multi-subunit assembly: multiple polypeptides forming one functional complex indicates quaternary structure.
  4. Distinguish denaturation: heat/solute can disrupt secondary/tertiary/quaternary interactions while leaving peptide bonds (primary structure) intact.
21. Why can burying hydrophobic side chains in the protein interior be thermodynamically favorable?
A. It increases the entropy of surrounding water molecules, contributing to a negative ΔG for folding
B. It breaks peptide bonds to reduce chain length and increase disorder
C. It requires ATP hydrolysis for every hydrophobic interaction formed
D. It eliminates all hydrogen bonding in the protein
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Hydrophobic collapse releases ordered water from hydrophobic surfaces, increasing water entropy and making folding more favorable (ΔG becomes more negative).
中: 疏水側鏈聚埋會釋放原本被迫有序排列嘅水分子，水熵↑，令摺疊 ΔG 更負、更自發。
22. The covalent bond formed after oxidation between two cysteine residues is a:
A. Salt bridge

C. Hydrogen bond
D. Peptide bond
✔️Correct!
❌Incorrect
Explanation / 解釋
EN: Oxidation of two cysteine thiols forms a covalent disulfide bond (cystine).
中: 兩個 Cys 的 –SH 氧化後形成 S–S 共價鍵（cystine），就係二硫鍵。
23. A protein composed of multiple polypeptide subunits interacting to form one functional complex is demonstrating:
A. Primary structure

Explanation / 解釋

中: 四級結構就係多條 polypeptide subunits 之間嘅組裝/互作形成一個功能蛋白。

中: 共軛蛋白會帶固定附著嘅非蛋白成分（prosthetic group），例如金屬離子/維生素/血紅素等。
25. Heating the enzyme causes loss of tertiary structure and activity while peptide bonds remain intact. This process is best termed:
