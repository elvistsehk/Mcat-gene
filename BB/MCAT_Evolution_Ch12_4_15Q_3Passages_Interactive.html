MCAT Biology — Evolution (Ch. 12.4)
Standalone Questions (Q1–Q15)
1. Which statement best distinguishes evolution from natural selection?
A. Evolution is a change in allele frequencies in a population over time; natural selection is one mechanism that can drive that change.
B. Evolution describes how individuals adapt during their lifetimes; natural selection describes how populations change.
C. Natural selection is a synonym for genetic drift; evolution is a synonym for mutation.
D. Evolution only occurs when new species form; natural selection only occurs within a species.
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Evolution is the broader outcome—changes in the gene pool of a population across generations. Natural selection is a process that can shift allele frequencies via differential reproductive success.

Other choices:

  • B: Individuals do not evolve genetically within a lifetime; populations do.
  • C: Genetic drift is random change in allele frequencies; mutation generates new alleles. Neither is synonymous with natural selection.
  • D: Evolution includes microevolution within species and macroevolution including speciation; natural selection can act at both scales.
2. The modern synthesis model (neo-Darwinism) most directly adds which idea to Darwin’s original framework?
A. Species are fixed and cannot change
B. Fossils show long periods of stasis with brief bursts of change
C. Heritable variation arises from genetic mechanisms (mutation and recombination) that alter the gene pool
D. Fitness is measured only by survival, not reproduction
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: The modern synthesis integrates Mendelian genetics and population genetics: mutation/recombination create variation and allele frequencies in populations change over time via selection and other forces.

Other choices:

  • A: Contradicts evolutionary theory.
  • B: Describes punctuated equilibrium, not the central addition of modern synthesis.
  • D: Fitness is defined by reproductive success (genetic contribution), not just survival.
3. Which scenario best illustrates inclusive fitness?
A. A predator population evolves sharper teeth because individuals “need” them
B. A mutation spreads because it increases the lifespan of individuals after reproduction
C. A population remains unchanged because no new mutations occur
D. An animal risks injury to help close relatives survive and reproduce, increasing shared genetic representation in future generations
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: Inclusive fitness considers an organism’s genetic success via its own offspring and by helping relatives (who share many genes) survive and reproduce.

Other choices:

  • A: Evolution is not goal-directed; “need” does not cause heritable change.
  • B: Post-reproductive lifespan effects can matter indirectly, but this is not clearly about boosting relatives’ reproduction.
  • C: Lack of mutation does not capture the idea of helping kin.
4. A fossil lineage shows long periods with little morphological change, followed by relatively rapid appearance of new forms. Which theory best explains this pattern?
A. Stabilizing selection
B. Punctuated equilibrium
C. Convergent evolution
D. Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: B.

Why: Punctuated equilibrium proposes evolutionary stasis for long intervals interrupted by bursts of rapid change, consistent with the described fossil pattern.

Other choices:

  • A: Stabilizing selection can maintain a mean phenotype but does not specifically predict sudden bursts.
  • C: Convergent evolution is similarity in unrelated lineages due to similar pressures, not a “stasis then bursts” tempo.
  • D: Hardy–Weinberg describes a non-evolving population’s allele frequencies under ideal conditions.
5. Which pattern of selection most directly reduces phenotypic variance by selecting against extremes?
A. Stabilizing selection
B. Directional selection
C. Disruptive selection
D. Divergent evolution
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Stabilizing selection favors intermediate phenotypes and selects against both extremes, narrowing the distribution.

Other choices:

  • B: Directional selection shifts the mean toward one extreme rather than narrowing around the mean.
  • C: Disruptive selection favors both extremes, often increasing variance or creating bimodality.
  • D: Divergent evolution describes divergence between species/lineages, not selection within a population distribution.
6. After an environmental change, individuals with one extreme phenotype have higher survival and reproduction, and the population mean shifts over generations. Which mode of selection is occurring?
A. Stabilizing selection
B. Disruptive selection
C. Directional selection
D. Parallel evolution
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Directional selection favors one extreme and shifts the population phenotype distribution toward that extreme over time.

Other choices:

  • A: Favors intermediates, not an extreme.
  • B: Favors both extremes simultaneously.
  • D: Parallel evolution refers to related species evolving similarly, not a selection mode within one population.
7. A population evolves two successful extreme phenotypes while intermediate individuals have lower fitness, producing a bimodal distribution. Which selection pattern best explains this?
A. Stabilizing selection
B. Directional selection
C. Genetic drift
D. Disruptive selection
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: Disruptive selection favors both extremes and selects against intermediates, often creating a bimodal phenotype distribution.

Other choices:

  • A: Would remove extremes and keep intermediates.
  • B: Would shift the whole distribution toward one side.
  • C: Drift is random and does not systematically favor extremes over intermediates.
8. Which statement best captures the biological species concept?
A. A species is any group of organisms that look alike
B. A species is the largest group of organisms capable of interbreeding to produce fertile offspring
C. A species is defined by occupying the same geographic region
D. A species is any lineage with the same number of chromosomes
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: B.

Why: The biological definition centers on reproductive compatibility and fertility of offspring.

Other choices:

  • A: Morphology can mislead (cryptic species; convergent evolution).
  • C: Many species overlap geographically without interbreeding.
  • D: Chromosome number alone is not sufficient (some different species share chromosome numbers; some same species show variation).
9. Two populations cannot mate because their reproductive anatomies are incompatible. This is an example of:
A. A prezygotic isolation mechanism
B. Hybrid sterility
C. Hybrid breakdown
D. A postzygotic isolation mechanism that occurs after zygote formation
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Mechanical (anatomical) incompatibility prevents fertilization, so the zygote never forms—prezygotic isolation.

Other choices:

  • B: Hybrid sterility happens after a viable hybrid is produced.
  • C: Hybrid breakdown refers to F1 hybrids being viable/fertile but later generations failing.
  • D: This contradicts the scenario (zygote never forms).
10. A horse and a donkey can produce a viable mule, but mules are sterile. This is best classified as:
A. Gametic isolation
B. Temporal isolation
C. Postzygotic isolation via hybrid sterility
D. Ecological isolation
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Fertilization occurs and the hybrid survives, but it cannot reproduce—classic postzygotic hybrid sterility.

Other choices:

  • A: Gametic isolation prevents fertilization in the first place.
  • B: Temporal isolation is breeding at different times, not sterility.
  • D: Ecological isolation is living in different niches within a region, preventing encounters.
11. Two unrelated lineages evolve similar body shapes because both adapt to a similar aquatic environment. Which pattern of evolution is illustrated?
A. Divergent evolution
B. Parallel evolution
C. Genetic drift
D. Convergent evolution
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: Convergent evolution is the independent evolution of similar traits in lineages that do not share a recent common ancestor, driven by similar selection pressures.

Other choices:

  • A: Divergent evolution is dissimilar traits arising from a shared ancestor.
  • B: Parallel evolution typically involves related species evolving similarly under analogous pressures.
  • C: Drift is random and not driven by the environment in a consistent, trait-specific way.
12. Two closely related species evolve similar traits over time while living in comparable environments. Which pattern best fits?
A. Convergent evolution
B. Parallel evolution
C. Hybrid breakdown
D. Stabilizing selection
✅Correct
❌Incorrect

Correct Answer: B.

Why: Parallel evolution involves related species evolving in similar ways under analogous environmental pressures.

Other choices:

  • A: Convergent evolution is usually emphasized for more distantly related lineages.
  • C: Hybrid breakdown refers to problems in later hybrid generations, not trait similarity evolution.
  • D: Stabilizing selection is a within-population selection mode, not a between-species pattern.
13. Two populations share a common ancestor but become increasingly dissimilar because they adapt to different environments. This is best described as:
A. Divergent evolution
B. Convergent evolution
C. Punctuated equilibrium
D. Directional selection
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Divergent evolution is the development of dissimilar traits in lineages sharing a common ancestor, usually due to different selection pressures.

Other choices:

  • B: Convergent evolution produces similarity, not dissimilarity.
  • C: Punctuated equilibrium is about tempo (rate pattern), not the direction of similarity/difference between lineages.
  • D: Directional selection is within a population; divergent evolution compares lineages/species.
14. Which statement best reflects how evolutionary change is measured at the genetic level over time?
A. Evolution is measured by how strongly individuals “want” to survive
B. Evolution is measured by the number of traits an organism acquires during its lifetime
C. Evolutionary rate can be inferred from changes in genotype/sequence over time, and greater genomic similarity suggests a more recent common ancestor
D. Evolutionary time is measured by the percent of proteins that contain sulfur
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Molecular comparisons (DNA sequence similarity) can be used as a molecular clock: more similarity generally implies a more recent split, while more differences imply more time since divergence.

Other choices:

  • A/B: Evolution is not driven by desire and is not measured by acquired traits.
  • D: Sulfur content is not a general measure of evolutionary time.
15. Which statement is most consistent with the idea that populations evolve rather than individuals?
A. An individual bacterium becomes resistant after “trying” to survive antibiotic exposure
B. A giraffe stretches its neck and passes the longer neck directly to its offspring
C. Individuals with the most offspring always live the longest
D. Over generations, alleles that increase reproductive success become more common in the population’s gene pool
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: The population’s allele frequencies shift across generations via differential reproduction. Individuals are selected, but the population evolves.

Other choices:

  • A/B: These are Lamarckian/teleological misconceptions; resistance and traits arise from heritable variation, not “trying” or stretching.
  • C: Fitness is about reproductive success, not necessarily longevity.
Passages (3 passages × 3 questions)
Passage 1: Selection in a Changing Environment

A coastal bird species feeds on snails. For decades, most snails had medium-thickness shells, and the birds’ average beak strength remained stable. A new crab species arrives and preferentially preys on snails with medium shells because they are easiest to crack. Very thin-shelled snails are crushed too quickly by the crab to be worth handling, while very thick-shelled snails take too long and the crab often abandons them.

Over the next 30 generations, field biologists document two peaks in shell thickness among the surviving snails: one peak at thin shells and one at thick shells. Birds that specialize on thin-shelled snails develop more slender beaks, while birds that specialize on thick-shelled snails develop stronger beaks. Intermediate beak morphologies become less common.

At the same time, the birds exhibit a behavior in which individuals give alarm calls that draw predator attention toward the caller, increasing the survival of nearby nestlings. The behavior is most frequent among individuals nesting near close relatives.

How to reason:

  1. Identify what happens to the distribution of phenotypes over time: one peak, shift, or two peaks?
  2. Two peaks with fewer intermediates points to disruptive selection rather than stabilizing or directional selection.
  3. For the alarm-call behavior, connect the cost to the individual with benefit to related offspring: that hints at inclusive fitness.
  4. Choose answers that integrate multiple details in the passage (predator preference + phenotype outcomes), not just a definition.
  5. Remember: selection acts on individuals, but the outcome is changing allele/trait frequencies in the population.
16. Which mode of selection best explains the emergence of two peaks in snail shell thickness after the crab’s arrival?
A. Disruptive selection
B. Stabilizing selection
C. Directional selection
D. Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: The crab selectively removes the intermediate shell phenotype while extremes persist, producing a bimodal distribution—classic disruptive selection.

Other choices:

  • B: Stabilizing selection would favor intermediates and eliminate extremes, the opposite of what’s observed.
  • C: Directional selection would shift the mean toward one extreme, not create two peaks.
  • D: Hardy–Weinberg describes conditions for no evolution; here the phenotype distribution is changing.

A. Individual birds evolved stronger beaks as a direct response to need within a single lifetime
B. Different beak traits were favored in different feeding contexts, shifting phenotype frequencies across generations
C. Mutation rates must have dropped to zero during the 30 generations
D. Birds and snails are now the same species because they interact ecologically
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: B.

Why: The passage describes differential survivorship and reproduction tied to feeding success, changing phenotype frequencies over generations—a population-level evolutionary response consistent with natural selection.

Other choices:

  • A: Teleological/Lamarckian; individuals do not genetically evolve “because they need to.”
  • C: Not implied and not required for selection to occur.
  • D: Species are defined by reproductive compatibility, not ecological interaction.
18. The alarm-call behavior occurring most often near close relatives is most directly explained by which concept?
A. Convergent evolution
B. Punctuated equilibrium
C. Inclusive fitness
D. Hybrid inviability
✅Correct
❌Incorrect

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Costly behavior that increases survival of nearby relatives can be favored because it increases the representation of shared genes in future generations (kin selection/inclusive fitness).

Other choices:

  • A: Convergent evolution is similarity in unrelated lineages, unrelated to altruistic behavior.
  • B: Punctuated equilibrium concerns tempo of evolution.
  • D: Hybrid inviability is a postzygotic reproductive barrier.
Passage 2: Speciation and Reproductive Barriers

Two populations of a salamander species become separated when a river changes course, creating a wide canyon. Over thousands of generations, the populations adapt to different microclimates on either side of the canyon. When researchers later relocate individuals into a shared enclosure, the salamanders frequently court, but mating attempts fail because the male’s clasping structures do not align properly with the female’s anatomy across the two populations.

In a follow-up study, scientists artificially bypass the mating barrier by manually combining gametes in vitro. The zygotes form and develop into juvenile salamanders, but the offspring are consistently unable to produce functional gametes as adults.

The researchers conclude that more than one barrier to gene flow has accumulated between the two populations over time.

How to reason:

  1. First, ask: does the barrier prevent zygote formation? If yes, it is prezygotic.
  2. Anatomical mismatch during mating attempts is a form of mechanical isolation (prezygotic).
  3. If a zygote forms but offspring cannot reproduce, that’s a postzygotic barrier (often hybrid sterility).
  4. Speciation is supported when multiple barriers reduce or prevent gene flow between populations.
  5. Pick the answer that matches the step in reproduction being blocked (before or after fertilization).

Correct Answer: A.

Why: The barrier occurs before fertilization (zygote never forms) and is due to anatomical incompatibility, a mechanical prezygotic barrier.

Other choices:

  • B/C: Those require fertilization and development to occur first.
  • D: Temporal isolation is breeding at different times; the passage describes failed physical alignment during mating attempts.

21. Which inference best supports the claim that speciation is underway between the two salamander populations?

❌Incorrect

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Speciation is supported by reproductive isolation. The passage gives both a prezygotic mechanical barrier and postzygotic sterility, strongly reducing gene flow.

  • B: Geographic separation reduces gene flow; it does not guarantee identical gene pools.

Passage 3: Similar Shapes, Different Histories

Biologists compare three animals that live in the ocean: a tuna, a shark, and a dolphin. All three have streamlined bodies and prominent tail structures used for propulsion. Genetic sequencing shows that dolphins share recent ancestry with other mammals, while tuna and sharks are both fish but are distantly related within vertebrates.

In a separate study, two closely related lizard species on different islands develop similar toe-pad structures that help them climb smooth leaves. The islands have comparable vegetation and predators. The two lizard species remain genetically close relative to each other despite living on different islands.

Finally, researchers compare DNA sequences between two bird species and find that they share 98% sequence identity across a set of conserved genes, while a different bird pair shares only 90% identity across the same genes. The team uses these comparisons to make inferences about relative divergence times.

How to reason:

  1. If unrelated (or not recently related) organisms become similar due to similar environments, that’s convergent evolution.
  2. If closely related species evolve similar adaptations under similar pressures, that suggests parallel evolution.
  3. For DNA similarity, more similarity generally implies a more recent split (molecular clock idea).
  4. Make sure to match each example to its relationship context (related vs not) plus the environmental pressure.
  5. Choose answers that explain the pattern using both the genetic evidence and the ecological story.
