üìò Passage 1 ‚Äî Dissecting lac Operon Control with Growth Conditions

Researchers measure lac structural gene transcription in E. coli under four growth conditions. They quantify lac mRNA by RT-qPCR after 10 minutes of exposure to each condition.

  3. Identify ‚Äúmaximal‚Äù: lactose present + low glucose gives both derepression and CAP activation ‚Üí highest output.
  4. Use the mutant: if CAP can‚Äôt bind, low-glucose advantage should largely disappear.

üìò Passage 2 ‚Äî Corepressor Logic in the trp Operon

A lab grows E. coli with an intact trp operon and measures transcription of tryptophan biosynthesis genes. In Medium 1, tryptophan is scarce and trp mRNA levels are high. In Medium 2, tryptophan is abundant and trp mRNA levels are very low.

Finally, the lab introduces a mutation in the repressor protein that prevents it from binding Trp or Trp* but does not affect its stability. How to reason (Passage 2):
  1. Identify the operon type: trp is negative repressible‚Äînormally on, turned off by product acting as corepressor.
  2. Use the analog logic: if Trp* binds the repressor, it should mimic high tryptophan and turn transcription off.
  3. Interpret the mutant: if the repressor can‚Äôt bind corepressor, it can‚Äôt be activated to bind the operator ‚Üí operon stays on.
  4. Avoid ‚Äúenzyme-style‚Äù traps: the analog‚Äôs inability to enter proteins is irrelevant if the regulatory step is binding the repressor.

  • C enhancers are eukaryotic; trp is bacterial operon control.

üìò Passage 3 ‚Äî Enhancers, Chromatin Remodeling, and Signal-Driven Expression

A research group studies a human gene, GENE-X, whose expression increases after exposure to a steroid hormone. They identify a DNA response element located ~700 bp upstream of the transcription start site. When hormone is added, a nuclear hormone receptor binds this element, and GENE-X mRNA rises ~8-fold within two hours.

When the response element is deleted, the hormone no longer increases GENE-X transcription, even though the promoter region remains intact. Chromatin immunoprecipitation (ChIP) shows that hormone treatment increases histone acetylation near the promoter and decreases nucleosome occupancy. Separately, treatment with an HDAC inhibitor increases baseline GENE-X transcription even without hormone, but the fold-change response to hormone becomes smaller (because baseline is already higher).

Finally, when DNA methylation is experimentally increased across the GENE-X locus, both baseline transcription and hormone responsiveness decrease, and the region shows hallmarks of more compact chromatin.

How to reason (Passage 3):

  2. Identify the trans factor: the hormone receptor is a trans regulator that binds the response element.
  3. Connect chromatin marks to access: histone acetylation ‚Üí more euchromatin ‚Üí higher transcription.
  4. Interpret drug logic: HDAC inhibitor raises baseline by keeping acetylation high; fold-change can shrink because you start higher.
  5. Use methylation clue: increased DNA methylation ‚Üí silencing/heterochromatin ‚Üí less transcription and responsiveness.
