MCAT Behavioral Sciences — Stress & Coping (Chapter 5.3)
Standalone Questions (Q1–Q15)
1. A student sees an unfamiliar exam prompt and immediately labels it as “dangerous” before thinking about solutions. Which appraisal stage is being demonstrated?
A. Secondary appraisal (coping-resource evaluation)
B. Primary appraisal (initial threat evaluation)
C. Problem-focused coping (directly changing the stressor)
D. Reappraisal (updating the stress level after coping)
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: B.

Why: Primary appraisal is the first, rapid evaluation of a situation’s meaning (irrelevant, benign–positive, or stressful). Labeling the situation as a threat happens before evaluating coping options.

Other choices: A is later and asks “Can I handle this?”; C is a coping strategy, not an appraisal stage; D is a subsequent reassessment that occurs after new information or coping attempts.

2. During a sudden loud alarm, which immediate physiological change is most directly driven by sympathetic nervous system activation?
A. Increased gastric motility and salivation
B. Decreased heart rate with peripheral vasodilation
C. Release of ACTH from the adrenal cortex
D. Increased heart rate with reduced digestion
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: The sympathetic “fight-or-flight” response increases cardiovascular output (e.g., heart rate) and diverts energy away from nonessential processes like digestion.

Other choices: A reflects parasympathetic “rest-and-digest”; B is opposite of SNS effects; C mixes up the endocrine axis—ACTH is released from the pituitary, not the adrenal cortex, and is not the most immediate SNS effect.

3. A person experiences stress while planning a wedding but describes it as exciting and motivating. This stressor is best classified as:
A. Eustress, because it is perceived as a positive challenge
B. Distress, because any life change is unpleasant
C. Harm, because damage has already occurred
D. Irrelevant appraisal, because weddings do not change physiology
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Eustress is stress experienced as positive (often a “challenge” appraisal) and can increase motivation even though it still requires adaptation.

Other choices: B is incorrect because stress can be positive; C is a component of secondary appraisal and implies actual damage; D contradicts the idea that lifestyle changes—even positive ones—can trigger physiological stress responses.

4. A firefighter reports high stress largely because each shift is unpredictable. Which psychological stress factor is most directly implicated?
A. Frustration
B. Pressure
C. Predictability
D. Approach–avoidance conflict
✅Correct
❌Incorrect

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Low predictability increases stress because the person cannot anticipate what demands will occur or plan coping responses in advance.

Other choices: A is blocked goal attainment; B is external demands/urgency; D is one option with both positive and negative outcomes (not primarily about uncertainty).

5. A patient evaluates a new diagnosis and thinks, “This could worsen, but I might also learn to manage it and become healthier.” Which secondary appraisal element is emphasized?
A. Harm only
B. Challenge (potential benefit through overcoming)
C. Irrelevant appraisal

Correct Answer: B.

Why: Secondary appraisal includes harm, threat, and challenge. Focusing on potential growth/benefit from coping reflects a challenge appraisal.

8. Which finding best fits the <i>exhaustion</i> stage of the general adaptation syndrome (GAS)?

Why: In exhaustion, the body can no longer sustain the elevated response; chronic activation contributes to illness risk, organ strain, and declining function.

Passages (3 passages × 3 questions each)
Passage 1: Appraisal Shifts During MCAT Preparation

A student begins a 10-week MCAT plan. In Week 1, the student sees a full-length practice exam score far below their goal and reports feeling “doomed.” They interpret the score as evidence that they are not capable of improving, and they spend the rest of the day ruminating. That night, sleep is poor and the next morning the student skips breakfast, reporting a “wired” feeling.

By Week 4, after joining a study group and tracking improvement, the student starts describing low scores as “feedback.” They still feel nervous before practice tests but now makes a concrete plan for reviewing missed questions. In the week before the real exam, the student feels energized and focused, but also notices more frequent colds when sleep falls below 6 hours for several nights.

How to reason:

  1. Identify primary vs secondary appraisal: primary labels the situation (irrelevant/benign–positive/stressful); secondary asks “Can I cope?” (harm/threat/challenge).
  2. Use the passage’s behavioral evidence (rumination vs planning, joining a study group, tracking improvement) to infer perceived coping resources.
  3. Connect symptoms to physiology: “wired,” poor sleep, skipped digestion-related behaviors → sympathetic activation; repeated short sleep + illness → chronic-stress immunosuppression risk.
16. Which interpretation best explains the student’s change from Week 1 to Week 4 using appraisal theory?
A. Primary appraisal changed from stressful to irrelevant, eliminating physiological stress responses
B. Secondary appraisal became more negative, increasing perceived harm and reducing coping
C. Primary appraisal remained benign–positive throughout, but coping strategies changed
D. Secondary appraisal shifted toward higher perceived coping and more challenge-oriented evaluation
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: D.

Why: Week 1 shows threat and low coping (“doomed,” rumination). By Week 4, joining a study group and making review plans indicates increased perceived coping resources and a more challenge-oriented frame—key features of secondary appraisal shifting.

Other choices: A is wrong because the exam is clearly still meaningful; B contradicts the improved planning/feedback mindset; C is inconsistent because Week 1 is not benign–positive.

17. In Week 1, which observation most directly supports that the student’s primary appraisal labeled the event as stressful rather than benign–positive?
A. They interpret the score as proof they cannot improve and feel “doomed”
B. They form a study group in Week 4
C. They track improvement across weeks
D. They feel energized in the week before the real exam
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: A.

Why: Primary appraisal is the initial meaning assigned to the event. “Doomed” and interpreting the score as incapacity indicates the event was appraised as threatening/stressful rather than beneficial.

Other choices: B–D occur later and reflect coping or later emotional states; they do not directly show the initial labeling of the event in Week 1.

18. Which mechanism best explains the student’s increased frequency of colds during the final week with repeated short sleep?
A. Acute stress primes immunity permanently, increasing infection risk
B. Epinephrine directly kills lymphocytes, causing immediate immunodeficiency
C. Chronic stress physiology (including prolonged cortisol elevation) can diminish immune responses
D. Primary appraisal automatically suppresses antibody production regardless of sleep
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: The passage links repeated inadequate sleep with getting sick more often. Chronic stress states—often accompanied by prolonged cortisol and other hormone changes—are associated with diminished immune response and higher illness susceptibility.

Other choices: A misstates acute stress effects (priming is temporary and not the same as chronic suppression); B overstates an immediate direct mechanism; D confuses cognition with endocrine/immune regulation.

Passage 2: Control, Pressure, and Performance Under Deadline

A research lab assigns two groups to complete the same complex data-analysis task. Group C is allowed to decide the order of subtasks, choose when to take breaks, and set intermediate milestones. Group P receives the same final deadline but is given strict instructions about task order and is told they may not deviate; progress will be checked at random times. Both groups are told the task affects their evaluation.

During the task, Group P reports feeling rushed and spends more time monitoring the time than checking for errors. Group C reports feeling busy but focused. At the end, both groups finish at similar times, but Group P shows higher self-reported irritability and makes more minor mistakes. A follow-up week later finds Group P also reports lower sleep quality despite no new tasks.

How to reason:

  1. Separate stressors: pressure (external demands/urgency) vs control (ability to influence the environment).
  2. Use performance patterns: time-checking and irritability suggest heightened stress; more errors can occur when cognitive resources shift to threat monitoring.
  3. Link persistent sleep issues after the task to a prolonged stress response (not necessarily ongoing danger, but ongoing arousal/reappraisal).
19. Which psychological stressor most directly differs between Group C and Group P, based on the passage design?
A. Frustration (blocked goals) is higher in Group C because they set their own milestones
B. Control is lower in Group P, which can increase stress even with the same deadline
C. Approach–approach conflict is higher in Group P because they have two desirable options
D. Inclement weather is higher in Group P due to random progress checks
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: B.

Why: Both groups face the same deadline (pressure), but Group P has restricted autonomy. Reduced control is a known psychological factor that increases stress, consistent with higher irritability and error patterns.

Other choices: A is unsupported (goals are not blocked); C mislabels the situation; D is irrelevant and not part of the experimental manipulation.

B. Lower predictability always increases dopamine, which directly causes calculation errors
C. Higher control in Group P reduced stress too much, lowering motivation and attention
D. Heightened stress can divert attentional resources toward threat/time monitoring rather than careful checking
✅Correct
❌Incorrect

Correct Answer: D.

Why: Group P’s rushed feelings and time monitoring suggest a stress-focused attentional shift. Under higher stress, cognitive resources may be allocated to monitoring demands and perceived threat, reducing careful error-checking and increasing minor mistakes.

Other choices: A incorrectly characterizes the conflict; B is an unsupported neurotransmitter claim; C reverses the group’s control manipulation and predicts the opposite direction.

Passage 3: Mapping Symptoms to Stages and Strategies

An emergency dispatcher works rotating shifts for six months. At first, each high-intensity call produces a burst of physical symptoms: rapid heartbeat, sweaty palms, and a sharp decrease in appetite during the shift. After several weeks, the dispatcher reports fewer obvious surges during calls but remains “on edge” for hours after work and has trouble unwinding. Blood pressure at clinic visits begins trending upward.

By month six, the dispatcher reports persistent fatigue, more frequent headaches, and catching respiratory infections more often than before the job. They also notice becoming irritable with family members and withdrawing socially. A supervisor recommends a structured program that includes diaphragmatic breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, and moderate exercise three times per week.

How to reason:

  1. Match time course to GAS: alarm = immediate fight-or-flight; resistance = sustained engagement/adaptation with ongoing hormone output; exhaustion = depleted resources + illness risk.
  2. Use the passage’s symptom pattern (early surges → later persistent high BP → later fatigue/infections) to identify stage transitions.
  3. Classify interventions: breathing/PMR/meditation are relaxation techniques; exercise can improve mood via endorphins and overall stress resilience.
22. The dispatcher’s month-six pattern (fatigue + infections + persistent symptoms) most strongly suggests which GAS stage?
A. Alarm, because symptoms are immediate and short-lived
B. Resistance, because all stress responses have fully resolved
C. Exhaustion, because prolonged stress is associated with illness susceptibility and declining reserves
D. Benign–positive appraisal, because the job is meaningful
✅Correct
❌Incorrect
Show explanation

Correct Answer: C.

Why: Month six includes fatigue, frequent infections, and persistent strain—consistent with exhaustion, when the body can no longer maintain elevated responses and becomes more vulnerable to illness and chronic conditions.

Other choices: A fits the first calls (acute surges), not month six; B contradicts persistent symptoms and rising blood pressure; D is an appraisal label, not a GAS stage.
